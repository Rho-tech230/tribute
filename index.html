More than a walking giant

He was a minister in the Eastern Nigerian government when our part of Cameroon was attached to Nigeria. After reunification in 1961 he became a minister in the Federal Republic of Cameroon. Back West of the Moungo, he held several ministerial posts in the Government of West Cameroon including that of Prime Minister. He later also served as President of the National Assembly of Cameroon.
Picture of ST Muna

Important Events in his Life

  • 1912 - born in Ngenmbo,Momo Division, NorthWest Cameroon
  • 1932 - 1947 - Director of several primary schools, then Head Tutor at the College of Teachers of Batibo . Pursues his higher education in London from where he graduated.
  • 1947-1951 - Teacher at Batibo.
  • 1951 - Elected to the Regional Assembly of Eastern Nigeria.
  • 1952-1954 -Becomes Minister of Public Works of Eastern Nigeria.
  • 1954-1957 - Separation of Eastern Nigeria and Western Cameroon , becomes Minister in charge of Resources and Public Works of Western Cameroon
  • 1959-1961 - Minister of Public Works then Minister of Finance, Trade and Industry.
  • January 11 1968 - May 20 1972 -Prime Minister of Western Cameroon. In June 1972 , the country put an end to the federal system and the post of Prime Minister remained vacant until 1975.
  • May 20 , 1972 - Minister of State.
  • 1981 - Was awarded the Bronze Wolf, the only distinction of the World Organization of the Scout Movement, awarded by the World Scout Committee for exceptional services to world Scouting.
  • June 14, 1973- 1988 - President of the National Assembly of Cameroon.
  • January 22 , 2002 - Death

Surely, Solomon Tandeng Muna will be remembered by different people for different things. I remember him for the phrase I once heard him utter as President of the National Assembly:People come and go, but the nation remains
